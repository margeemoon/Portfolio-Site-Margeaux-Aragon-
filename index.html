Hi, my name is Margeaux Aragon

I'm a UI/ UX Designer from Los Angeles!

I’m a UI/ UX and Product Designer with a background in food, specializing in baking and pastry. I spent most of my career leading multiple teams of young pastry cooks, executing menus designed by myself with the collaboration of my colleagues. I have worked for some of the biggest names in food and my greatest achievement was a published recipe in Nancy Silverton's Mozza at Home cookbook! Because of my love for food, I found a love for traveling. Going to different countries, cities, provencial towns and eating my way through it's culture is one of my most favorite things to do! I think food and what I have learned from my experiences is at the heart of my design solutions. I have a passion for teaching, creating, and pleasing a multitude of palates. With my previous experience as a leader, I would make a great addition to any team. I understand the need for oranization, procedure, testing, communication, and learning. The same principles apply to my role as a UI/UX Designer, I need to know the process of the inventions in order to optimize the product.

MARCHÉ MANGE

An ecommerce brand that brings the bodega to you. With a global pandemic already under way we at MARCHÉ MANGE know how tough it can be to get the products you need, so we specialize in artesinal food products, locally and organically grown produce, ready-to-eat healthy meals, a few bar products and kitchens tools to bring the restaurant to you!

For this project I used pen and paper to create low-fidelity wireframes. Then Sketch to build mid-fidelity frames; a prototype using InVision was created to conduct user testing to complete a set of two tasks. From there, the highh-fidelity wireframes were created and finished again, in Sketch.

Talk Talk

I began my research by reviewing other messaging apps. Predominantly Facebook Messanger. I loved the simplicity but I wanted to make my app a bit more simple with a fun design. Then I found a design on Dribbble by a designer called SoLemon that I wanted to mimic and Talk Talk was born.

For this project, I started to design immediately. I did not sketch out any wireframes nor did I begin this process in the traditional sense. I thought of this task as a fun refresher after creating an extensive to-do list wallet app so instead, I had fun and went straight to Sketch and started to play around. From there, I created a UI kit containg reusable UI elements and typography for any future iterations and new screen developments from my team.
